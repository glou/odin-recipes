Croque Monsieur

Description

A croque monsieur is a hot sandwich made with ham and cheese. The dish originated in French cafés and bars as a quick snack.

The name comes from the French words croque ("bite”) and monsieur ("mister").

Source: Wikipedia

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 pound shredded Gruyere cheese, divided
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 16 slices white sandwich bread
  • ½ pound thinly sliced ham

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C). Place bread slices on a baking sheet.
  2. Melt butter in a saucepan. Add flour; cook and stir until paste-like, about 2 minutes. Slowly add milk, while stirring. Cook and stir until sauce is thickened, about 3 minutes. Turn off the burner and stir in 1/2 cup Gruyere cheese, salt, and pepper.
  3. Toast bread slices in the preheated oven, keeping an eye on them so they don't burn, 3 to 4 minutes per side.
  4. Remove bread from the oven and top 8 slices with ham. Sprinkle 1/2 of the remaining Gruyere over the ham, then top with remaining 8 bread slices. Pour Bechamel sauce over each sandwich and sprinkle with remaining Gruyere.
  5. Return to the oven and bake for 5 minutes. Turn on the broiler and broil until cheese is nicely toasted but not burned, 1 to 2 minutes. Enjoy while hot.
